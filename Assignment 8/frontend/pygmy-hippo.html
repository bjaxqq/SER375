Pygmy Hippo

Choeropsis liberiensis

About

The Pygmy Hippo (Choeropsis liberiensis) is a small species of hippo native to the forests and swamps of West Africa. Unlike its larger relatives, the pygmy hippo is more solitary and spends a significant amount of time on land.

These herbivores are nocturnal, emerging at night to graze on grass, fruit, and plants. Pygmy hippos are endangered due to habitat loss and hunting.
