Narwhal

About

The Narwhal (Monodon monoceros) is a unique marine mammal found in the Arctic waters. Known for its long, spiral tusk, the narwhal is often referred to as the "unicorn of the sea." These creatures are well adapted to life in cold waters.

Narwhals primarily feed on fish, squid, and shrimp, and are important to the marine ecosystem. They are known to communicate through clicks, whistles, and other sounds.
